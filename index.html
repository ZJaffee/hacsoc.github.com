hacker, n: One who is intensely interested in learning and modifying the technical aspects of a system, particularly a computer and its software; one who hacks; a computer programmer who is largely or completely self-taught.

CWRU Hacker Society is a common ground for those who are interested in digging into the innards of things; for those who use, produce, or support open-source software (or hardware); and for those who would like to learn more about open-source development.

Meetings

Hacker Society currently meets regularly on Wednesdays at 7:30 PM in Glennan 421 for tech talks and round table discussions. Students are also invited to work on personal projects on Saturdays from 2:00-4:00pm in the Glennan student lounge. Subscribe to our mailing list to be updated weekly with dates, times, and topics.

Maintainers

The current maintainer of Hacker Society is Adam Gleischner. Previous maintainers include Jon Pfeil and Brendan Higgins. Finally, Tim Henderson is the maintainer emeritus.
